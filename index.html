DESIGN PROCESS

  • Customize your plan with an Architect

    The beginning of your home is determined by four simple factors: your lot size, neighborhood restrictions, your budget and lifestyle needs. Based on this information, we’re able to align you with one of our base designs meant to satisfy your needs. Not a perfect fit? No problem. Included in our price is the opportunity to work with our Architects to further customize the floor plans to fit you. We’ve seen some really exciting changes including adding 5th bedrooms, extending the outdoor living space, and attic game-rooms. Any additions to the base house are then quickly priced so we can see how they affect your overall budget. This ensures that your home is unique to you and most importantly, on budget.

  • Phase 1: Design and Estimating
    Initial Consultation :

    During the initial consultation, we discuss your design requirements, ideas and finish levels. We also assess your site (lot) and inspect the existing structure.

  • Conceptual Design and Preliminary Budget :

    Based on the information gathered during the initial consultation, a conceptual design and preliminary budget is developed. The design and budget will be refined until you have a design concept and budget that fits you.

  • Estimating and Bid Conference :

    Once an approved design and budget is established, we conduct a formalized bidding process during which we meet with all subcontractors, suppliers and tradesmen to refine the cost and provide you with a fixed price contract.

  • Fixed Price Proposal and Contract Approval :

    At this stage, we present you a fixed price proposal, detailed project specifications and a finalized conceptual design for your approval.

  • Phase 2: Preconstruction
    Architectural/Engineering :

    The conceptual design is used to develop the working drawings or , as they are commonly called, the “blue prints”. Site surveys are ordered and the structural portions of the project are engineered.

  • Color Session :

    During the “color session” you will work with our design team in our design center to select the colors, textures and finishes of your home.

  • Red Line Meeting :

    Once the plans have been developed, we schedule a plan review meeting with you or as we call it, the “red line” meeting. During this meeting, all details of the plans are explained to you in layman terms and any deviations or changes to the plans are made.

  • Permitting and Approvals :

    Once you have approved the final plan, we start the process of obtaining the building permit and/or any neighborhood approvals. This is the last step before construction.

  • Phase 3: Construction
    Start of Construction :

    The start of construction is an exciting time but before we begin, we take steps on your behalf to minimize the overall impact of the project. We carefully consider how your home is to be protected, how workers will enter and exit your home, the location of the dumpster, and any other consideration that should be considered. We build a plan around you and your family’s lifestyle.
